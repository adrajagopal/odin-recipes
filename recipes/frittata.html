My Frittata Recipe

Description

Frittata makes for a great breakfast, especially when you have a group to feed! Frittatas are simple to make - all you need is a few vegetables, eggs, milk (or a substitute), spices/seasonings and a pan. You start by sautéing the vegetables, then add in the egg mixture, and finally just bake it.

In total, it'll take about an hour to make a frittata. You'll need time to prep and sauté the vegetables, let the egg mixture cook a little on the stove, and then about 30 minutes of baking time. When I make frittatas, I put hot sauce on top and serve alongside some hash browns. Enjoy the recipe!

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1 white onion, cut into thin slivers
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of raw spinach
  • 2 campari tomatoes, diced OR 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1 medium-sized sweet potato, chopped into small pieces
  • 2 cups of broccoli, chopped into small pieces
  • 5 eggs OR equivalent Just Egg
  • 1/4 cup of milk or milk substitute
  • some amount of avocado oil to sautee the vegetables (you don't need a lot)

Seasonings (add to taste)

  • salt
  • sugar (optional)
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • smoked paprika
  • cumin powder
  • chili powder (optional)
  • garlic powder (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat a 9-inch oven-safe pan on medium heat and add oil, and preheat your oven to 425F
  2. Mix the eggs and milk (or substitute) into a pouring cup, and whip until thoroughly mixed. Set aside
  3. Sauté onions until soft and translucent. Adding salt and sugar makes the onions taste great
  4. When the onions are almost done, add the garlic and toss for a couple of minutes
  5. Add the sweet potato and broccoli, and cover for a few minutes until the sweet potato is soft. Stir occasionally
  6. Finally, add the tomatoes and spinach. Cover again to steam the spinach and cook down. Stir occasionally
  7. Add the rest of your seasonings to the pan, stir and cook
  8. When the vegetables are cooked to your liking, add in the egg mixture. Pour evenly and tilt the pan to ensure even coverage
  9. Turn the stove off, but leave the pan on the range for 3-5 minutes to lightly cook the egg
  10. Place the pan in the oven and bake for roughly 8-10 minutes
  11. Check occassionally, by sticking a fork in the frittata. If the fork comes out wet, keep cooking. Stop cooking when the fork comes out clean

Cut into pie slices, let cool and enjoy!
